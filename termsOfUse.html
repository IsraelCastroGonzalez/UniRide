Information we collect

When you use Uniride we ask for your name and email address. We use this inside the app, in notification emails to you and fellow commenters, and for authenticating your identity to log you into your account. Like most websites on the Internet, we collect non-personal information that your web browser makes available: your browser type, the link that linked to Uniride, your city, and the date and time of your visit. We use this information for research to understand how our users on average use Uniride and how we can make it better. We don’t share this information or your designs or use it for any other purpose (except to comply with a subpoena under the law.) We will not rent or sell this information or your designs to anyone.

Cookies

A cookie is a small amount of data Uniride saves on your computer. We use the cookie to recognise you when you visit Uniride in the future. The cookie contains an encrypted, anonymised user ID. Uniride reads the user ID, matches it with the ID stored in our database, and recalls your name, email, colour and recently uploaded designs from our database. Your personal information is not stored on your computer in the cookie. No other websites are able to access the Uniride cookie. If you dislike this, your browser has a function to clear cookies— if you do that, Uniride won’t be able to recognise your computer in the future.

Uniride staff have agreed not to disclose personal or anonymous information to others. You can delete your profile at any time by clicking the “Delete this design” link at the bottom of the profile page. The profile and all comments on it will immediately become unviewable by everyone including us. We claim no intellectual property rights over the profile you upload to Uniride.
